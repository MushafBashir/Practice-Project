THOMAS EDISON

Thomas Edison (1847-1931) was an iconic American inventor and businessman who significantly influenced the course of modern history with his numerous groundbreaking innovations. Known as "The Wizard of Menlo Park," he held over 1,000 patents, showcasing his remarkable creativity and entrepreneurial spirit. Edison's most renowned invention was the practical electric light bulb, which revolutionized society by transforming the way people lived and worked. His relentless experimentation and dedication to research led to advancements in various fields, such as motion pictures, sound recording, and telecommunications. Beyond his inventions, Edison's enduring legacy lies in his commitment to the process of innovation, epitomized by his famous quote: "Genius is one percent inspiration and ninety-nine percent perspiration." His profound impact on technology and the modern world continues to be celebrated and admired to this day.
