BOOKER T.WASHINGTON

Booker T. Washington, born on April 5, 1856, and passing away on November 14, 1915, was a prominent African-American educator, author, and leader. He rose from slavery to become a driving force in the advancement of black education and economic empowerment in the post-Civil War United States. His emphasis on vocational training and self-reliance through his renowned Tuskegee Institute greatly impacted the socio-economic progress of African-Americans during a challenging period in American history.
