MOTHER TERESA

Mother Teresa, born on August 26, 1910, and passing away on September 5, 1997, was a renowned Albanian-Indian Catholic nun and missionary. Her life's work was dedicated to serving the poor and destitute in Kolkata, India, founding the Missionaries of Charity in 1950 to provide assistance, comfort, and hope to the marginalized and suffering across the world. Her selfless devotion and compassion have left an indelible mark on global humanitarian efforts.
