ANDREW CARNEGIE

Andrew Carnegie was a Scottish-American industrialist and philanthropist who led the expansion of the American steel industry in the late 19th century. He was also one of the most important benefactors of his era, donating millions of dollars to various causes such as education, peace, and science. He was born in Dunfermline, Scotland in 1835 and immigrated to the United States with his family in 1848. He started his career as a telegrapher and later became a successful entrepreneur and investor in various industries.
